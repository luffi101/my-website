Name: Leif Erikson
Date of Birth - Date of Death: c. 970 – c. 1020
Nationality: Norse (Icelandic/Norwegian)
Region of the world: Northern Europe, North Atlantic
Description of his significance:
Considered by many historians as the first European to set foot in North America (Vinland), Leif Erikson opened the way for later transatlantic voyages. His journey predated Christopher Columbus by nearly five centuries.

---
Name: Ibn Battuta
Date of Birth - Date of Death: 24 February 1304 – 1368/1369 (uncertain)
Nationality: Moroccan
Region of the world: North Africa, Middle East, Asia
Description of his significance:
Ibn Battuta traveled for nearly 30 years across the Islamic world, as well as parts of Africa, Eastern Europe, and Asia. His extensive accounts, recorded in the Rihla, provide invaluable insight into the 14th-century cultures and geography he encountered.

---
Name: Marco Polo
Date of Birth - Date of Death: 15 September 1254 – 8 January 1324
Nationality: Venetian (Italian)
Region of the world: Europe, Asia
Description of his significance:
Marco Polo’s travels to Central Asia and China, documented in The Travels of Marco Polo, greatly influenced European knowledge of the East. His detailed descriptions of the wealth and sophistication of the Mongol Empire spurred further exploration.

---
Name: Zheng He
Date of Birth - Date of Death: 1371 – 1433/1435 (uncertain)
Nationality: Chinese
Region of the world: East Asia, Indian Ocean, Middle East, East Africa
Description of his significance:
A Chinese admiral and diplomat during the Ming Dynasty, Zheng He led grand maritime expeditions across the Indian Ocean, reaching as far as the coast of East Africa. His fleet showcased China’s naval power and facilitated cultural and commercial exchange.

---
Name: Christopher Columbus
Date of Birth - Date of Death: 1451 – 20 May 1506
Nationality: Italian (Genoese)
Region of the world: Europe, Atlantic, the Americas
Description of his significance:
Columbus’s voyages, sponsored by the Spanish Crown, initiated sustained European contact with the Americas. Though he did not realize he had reached a new continent, his expeditions altered global trade, politics, and cultural exchange.

---
Name: Vasco da Gama
Date of Birth - Date of Death: c. 1460s – 24 December 1524
Nationality: Portuguese
Region of the world: Europe, Africa, Indian Ocean
Description of his significance:
Da Gama was the first European to find a sea route from Europe to India around the Cape of Good Hope. His voyages allowed Portugal to establish a long-lasting colonial and commercial empire in the Indian Ocean region.

---
Name: Ferdinand Magellan
Date of Birth - Date of Death: 4 February 1480 – 27 April 1521
Nationality: Portuguese (sailed under Spanish crown)
Region of the world: Europe, Atlantic, Pacific
Description of his significance:
Magellan led the first expedition to circumnavigate the globe, proving the world’s roundness in practical terms. Though he was killed in the Philippines, part of his crew completed the voyage, altering European understanding of world geography.

---
Name: Captain James Cook
Date of Birth - Date of Death: 27 October 1728 – 14 February 1779
Nationality: English
Region of the world: Europe, Pacific, Atlantic
Description of his significance:
Cook’s three major voyages vastly expanded European knowledge of the Pacific. He charted large swaths of Oceania, including Australia, New Zealand, and the Hawaiian Islands, and his meticulous cartography improved naval navigation.

---
Name: Alexander von Humboldt
Date of Birth - Date of Death: 14 September 1769 – 6 May 1859
Nationality: Prussian (German)
Region of the world: Europe, South America
Description of his significance:
A polymath who traveled extensively in Latin America, Humboldt’s scientific observations on geography, climate, and ecology laid foundations for modern fields like biogeography and environmental science. His work profoundly influenced later explorers and naturalists.

---
Name: David Livingstone
Date of Birth - Date of Death: 19 March 1813 – 1 May 1873
Nationality: Scottish
Region of the world: Europe, Africa
Description of his significance:
Livingstone was a missionary and explorer who conducted extensive expeditions in Africa, mapping unknown regions and searching for the source of the Nile. His reports brought global attention to Africa’s geography and the horrors of the slave trade.
