Name: Prince Henry the Navigator (Infante Dom Henrique)
Date of Birth - Date of Death: 4 March 1394 – 13 November 1460
Nationality: Portuguese
Region of the world: Europe, Atlantic
Description of his significance:
Though he did not personally sail on many major voyages, Prince Henry financed and supported numerous expeditions along the West African coast. His patronage was crucial in ushering in the Age of Discovery, leading Portugal to become a global maritime power.

---
Name: Bartolomeu Dias
Date of Birth - Date of Death: c. 1450 – 29 May 1500
Nationality: Portuguese
Region of the world: Europe, Africa
Description of his significance:
Dias led the first European expedition to round the Cape of Good Hope (1488), opening up the sea route from Europe to Asia. This milestone paved the way for future exploration and the Portuguese spice trade in the Indian Ocean.

---
Name: Christopher Columbus
Date of Birth - Date of Death: 1451 – 20 May 1506
Nationality: Italian (Genoese)
Region of the world: Europe, Atlantic, the Americas
Description of his significance:
Columbus’s voyages, sponsored by the Catholic Monarchs of Spain, initiated sustained European contact with the Americas. Although he never realized he had reached a new continent, his expeditions profoundly impacted global trade, geopolitics, and cultural exchange.

---
Name: Amerigo Vespucci
Date of Birth - Date of Death: 9 March 1454 – 22 February 1512
Nationality: Italian (Florentine)
Region of the world: Europe, Atlantic, the Americas
Description of his significance:
Vespucci explored the coast of South America and was among the first to suggest that Columbus had reached entirely new continents. The name “America” is derived from a Latinized form of his first name.

---
Name: John Cabot (Giovanni Caboto)
Date of Birth - Date of Death: c. 1450 – c. 1499
Nationality: Italian (Venetian), sailed under English sponsorship
Region of the world: Europe, North Atlantic
Description of his significance:
Commissioned by King Henry VII of England, Cabot’s 1497 voyage across the North Atlantic likely landed on the coast of North America (Newfoundland). His expedition laid groundwork for later British claims in the New World.

---
Name: Sir Francis Drake
Date of Birth - Date of Death: c. 1540 – 28 January 1596
Nationality: English
Region of the world: Europe, Atlantic, Pacific
Description of his significance:
Drake was the first Englishman to circumnavigate the globe (1577–1580). He carried out numerous raids against Spanish possessions in the New World and played a key role in defending England against the Spanish Armada.

---
Name: Jacques Cartier
Date of Birth - Date of Death: 31 December 1491 – 1 September 1557
Nationality: French
Region of the world: Europe, North America
Description of his significance:
Cartier made three voyages to what is now Canada (1534–1542), exploring the Gulf of St. Lawrence and the St. Lawrence River. His expeditions laid the foundation for French claims in North America.

---
Name: Abel Tasman
Date of Birth - Date of Death: 1603 – 10 October 1659
Nationality: Dutch
Region of the world: Europe, Asia-Pacific
Description of his significance:
Tasman explored the Pacific on behalf of the Dutch East India Company. He was the first known European to reach Tasmania (now named after him) and New Zealand, greatly expanding European knowledge of Australia and surrounding regions.

---
Name: Vitus Bering
Date of Birth - Date of Death: 1681 – 19 December 1741
Nationality: Danish (in service of the Russian Empire)
Region of the world: Europe, Arctic, North Pacific
Description of his significance:
Commissioned by the Russian Empire, Bering sailed through the strait now bearing his name, proving Asia and North America were not connected by land. His explorations spurred Russian expansion into Siberia and Alaska.

---
Name: Henry Hudson
Date of Birth - Date of Death: c. 1565 – c. 1611
Nationality: English
Region of the world: Europe, North Atlantic
Description of his significance:
Hudson explored parts of the Arctic Ocean and the river that now bears his name, the Hudson River. His voyages provided vital information about northern waterways and prompted further Dutch and English colonization in North America.

---
Name: Henry Morton Stanley
Date of Birth - Date of Death: 28 January 1841 – 10 May 1904
Nationality: Welsh-American
Region of the world: Europe, Africa
Description of his significance:
Stanley became famous for his exploration of Central Africa and for finding the missing missionary-explorer David Livingstone. He mapped parts of the Congo River basin, paving the way for European colonial expansion in that region.
